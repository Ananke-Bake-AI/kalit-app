Here’s my honest take: the page already has good ambition and energy, but right now it reads more like an AI hype landing page than a clear high-conviction suite website for founders and businesses. The biggest issue is not design — it’s positioning clarity. The hero says “Build Anything Run Everything” and “the world’s most advanced AI agent models,” while the rest of the page presents Kalit as a suite with 4 concrete products: Pentest, Flow, Marketing, and Project. Pentest: secure systems

Flow: websites / landing pages

Marketing: acquisition / conversions

Project: prompt to deployed app 

You also already have multiple action-oriented CTAs like “Start my project,” “Create my landing,” “Start my campaign,” and “Launch my project,” which is good because they map to user intent better than one generic button. 

What is hurting conversion
1. The hero is too broad
Your current hero says:

“Build Anything Run Everything”

“Let Kalit build your team using the world’s most advanced AI agent models…” 

That sounds powerful, but it does not immediately answer:

Who is this for?

What exactly can I buy?

Why should I trust it?

What happens after I click?

build my app

launch my landing page

run my marketing

secure my product

“The most powerful AI platform”

“No limits”

“Only results”

“Dominate the market”

“The only platform that delivers everything”
